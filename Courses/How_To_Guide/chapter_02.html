2.1 Starting a Quiz

Each course has a Practice Quiz linked from the cross-nav Chapter dropdown and from the course card on the home page. Clicking it takes you to the setup screen, where you configure the quiz before starting.

On the setup screen you will find:

  • Question pool count — shows the total number of questions available across all chapters.
  • Mode selection — choose Standard or Weakness Focus (covered in Section 2.3).
  • Chapter filter chips — if the course has more than one chapter, toggleable chips let you include or exclude each chapter's questions. Only questions from the toggled-on chapters will appear in your quiz.
  • Start Quiz — begins a quiz with up to 10 random questions from the selected chapters using whichever mode is active.
  • Randomize — a shortcut that switches to Standard mode and starts immediately, bypassing mode selection.

Each quiz session draws up to 10 questions. If the filtered pool has fewer than 10, all of them are used.

2.2 Question Types

Quiz questions come in two formats:

Multiple Choice (MC) — four options labeled A–D (sometimes three). Click the radio button next to your choice, then click Check Answer. The button is disabled until you make a selection. After checking:

  • Your choice turns green if correct, red if wrong.
  • The correct answer is highlighted green.
  • An explanation appears below the choices, describing why the correct answer is right.
  • Some wrong choices also show a Common mistake panel explaining the specific misconception.
  • A Hint button may appear if the question has a formula — click it to reveal the formula before answering.

Text Input — a text box where you type your answer, then click Check Answer. Rules for matching:

  • Comparisons are case-insensitive and trimmed (extra spaces are ignored).
  • Numeric answers may have a small tolerance (e.g., ±0.01) to allow for rounding differences.
  • The engine automatically detects off-by-a-factor-of-ten errors and shows a specific hint for them.
  • Some questions have a list of known wrong answers with tailored hints to explain the mistake.

You can use the Prev and Next buttons to navigate between questions. The live score (e.g., "Score: 4/6") is always visible at the top of the quiz screen.

2.3 Modes & Progress Tracking

The quiz engine has two modes:

  • Standard mode — draws 10 questions at random from the selected chapters' pool, with equal probability for each question.
  • Weakness Focus mode (⚡) — reads your stored quiz history and weights the random selection toward questions you have answered incorrectly more often. It targets your weakest areas automatically.

After completing a quiz, the results screen shows:

  • Your score (e.g., 7/10) and a performance label.
  • A Questions to Review section listing every question you got wrong, with the correct answer shown.
  • A Quiz History panel at the bottom showing your last several attempts with dates and scores.

From the results screen you can:

  • Back — return to the setup screen.
  • Try Again — retake the exact same question set.
  • New Random — start a new random Standard-mode quiz.
  • ⚡ Weakness Focus — jump straight into a weakness-targeted session.

All quiz data — scores, wrong-answer counts, and history — is stored in your browser's localStorage. No internet connection or account is needed. Note: clearing your browser's site data will erase this history.
