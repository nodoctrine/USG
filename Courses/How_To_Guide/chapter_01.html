1.1 What Is This Study Guide?

The Universal Study Guide is a self-contained, interactive textbook that runs entirely in your browser. There is no server, no account, and no internet connection required after you have the files.

Content is organized into courses — like Electronics Basics or C Programming. Each course is broken into chapters with reading material and activities, plus an optional practice quiz.

Key facts about the system:

  • Progress saves automatically — every activity you complete is stored in your browser's local storage. It persists between sessions as long as you use the same browser.
  • Works offline — all files are local. Open the folder, double-click index.html, and you are ready.
  • Three themes — Light, Dark, and Night Shift (a warm amber tone). Your preference is remembered across all pages.
  • No penalties — activities and quizzes are for practice. Wrong answers provide explanations, not deductions.

This guide (the How-To Guide) explains how the system works. Reading it first will help you get the most out of every other course.

1.2 The Interface

The chapter interface has four main areas:

  • Page header — fixed at the top. Shows the course name, chapter title, a progress pill ("X% complete"), a remaining pill ("N remaining"), the theme switcher, and a ? button for the UI tour.
  • Cross-nav bar — below the header. Has three controls: a home link (← All Courses), a Course dropdown, and a Chapter dropdown. These let you jump anywhere without backtracking.
  • Sidebar — left panel (collapses on small screens). Lists every section with a completion counter (e.g., 1/3). Clicking a label scrolls you to that section. Reset buttons at the bottom let you clear progress for this chapter or the entire course.
  • Main content area — the reading and activity blocks that make up the chapter content.

A thin progress bar runs across the full width of the page just below the cross-nav. It fills from left to right as you complete activities. When the chapter is 100% complete, a green completion banner appears with a link to the next chapter.

1.3 Activities & Completion

Each chapter page contains two types of content blocks:

  • Reading blocks — plain text, diagrams, and bullet lists. No action required; just read. They do not appear in the progress count.
  • Activity blocks — interactive. Each one has a completion chevron (○ → ✓) in the top-right corner that flips when you finish.

The three activity types you will encounter:

  • Animation — step-by-step visual walkthrough. Use the Prev / Next buttons to move through frames. You must reach the final frame to mark it complete.
  • Multiple Choice (MC) — select one radio button per question. The block marks done when every question in it has been answered. Explanations appear immediately after answering.
  • Short Answer (SA) — type a word, phrase, or number. Click Check, or click Show Answer to reveal the correct response (also marks the block done).

When you return to a chapter you visited before, your previous typed answers are cleared — but the completion checkmarks remain. This is intentional: it encourages you to actively recall the answers again rather than just reading what you typed last time.
